# Sales Data Analysis Using SQL

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing the Superstore Sales Dataset using SQL. The objective is to explore the dataset, apply filtering techniques, perform aggregations, identify top-performing products and customers, analyze regional performance, and generate business insights through SQL queries.

The analysis was performed using MySQL Workbench and covers essential SQL concepts such as filtering, grouping, sorting, aggregation, trend analysis, and data validation.

---

## Tools & Technologies

- MySQL Workbench
- SQL
- Superstore Dataset

---

## Project Objectives

- Explore and understand the sales dataset.
- Apply filtering using the WHERE clause.
- Perform aggregations using SQL functions.
- Analyze category-wise and region-wise sales performance.
- Identify top-selling products and top customers.
- Analyze monthly sales and profit trends.
- Validate data quality through duplicate and null-value checks.

---

## SQL Operations Performed

### 1. Data Exploration

The dataset was explored to understand its structure and contents.

Queries Performed:
- View first 10 records
- Count total records
- View table structure
- Identify distinct product categories

### Key Insight

The dataset contains customer, product, sales, quantity, profit, and regional information, making it suitable for business performance analysis.

---

### 2. Data Filtering

Filtering was performed to analyze specific business segments.

Queries Performed:
- Orders from West Region
- Technology category products
- Orders with Sales greater than 1000
- Furniture orders from East Region

### Key Insight

Filtering helps identify high-value transactions, region-specific performance, and category-specific business opportunities.

---

### 3. Aggregation Analysis

Aggregate functions were used to calculate overall business metrics.

Queries Performed:
- Total Sales
- Average Sales
- Total Quantity Sold
- Total Profit

### Key Insight

Aggregation provides a summary of overall business performance and helps evaluate revenue generation and profitability.

---

### 4. Category Analysis

Category-level analysis was performed using GROUP BY.

Queries Performed:
- Total Sales by Category
- Total Profit by Category

### Key Insight

Different product categories contribute differently to revenue and profitability. This analysis helps identify high-performing categories.

---

### 5. Product Analysis

Top-performing products were identified based on total sales.

Query Performed:
- Top 10 Products by Sales

### Key Insight

A small number of products generate a significant portion of total revenue and should receive priority in inventory planning.

---

### 6. Customer Analysis

Customer-wise sales analysis was conducted.

Query Performed:
- Top 10 Customers by Sales

### Key Insight

Top customers contribute a substantial share of business revenue and can be targeted through retention and loyalty programs.

---

### 7. Regional Analysis

Regional sales performance was evaluated.

Query Performed:
- Sales by Region

### Key Insight

Sales vary across regions, indicating differences in customer demand and market performance.

---

### 8. Business Use Cases

Business-oriented SQL queries were used to identify trends and validate data quality.

Queries Performed:
- Monthly Sales Trend
- Monthly Profit Trend
- Duplicate Order ID Detection
- Missing Sales Value Check

### Key Insights

- Monthly trends help identify seasonal patterns and demand fluctuations.
- Profit trends may differ from sales trends, highlighting margin variations.
- Duplicate Order IDs indicate multiple products within the same order or require further validation.
- Null-value checks help maintain data accuracy and reporting reliability.

---

## Business Findings

- Sales performance differs significantly across categories and regions.
- Technology products generally contribute a large share of revenue.
- Certain customers generate substantially higher sales than others.
- Monthly sales and profit trends reveal important business patterns.
- Data validation ensures reliable and accurate analysis results.

---

## Conclusion

The Superstore dataset was successfully analyzed using SQL. Various SQL operations including filtering, aggregation, grouping, sorting, trend analysis, and data validation were applied to generate meaningful business insights.

The project demonstrates how SQL can transform raw sales data into actionable information for decision-making. The findings can support inventory management, customer relationship strategies, regional planning, and overall business growth.
